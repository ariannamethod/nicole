## ðŸ“š GRAMMAR RULES (NOT TEMPLATES!)

### What Are Grammar Rules?

Grammar rules are the **syntax of language**:
- Subject-verb agreement (I am, he is, they are)
- Capitalization (first word of sentence)
- Article usage (a/an/the)
- Sentence structure (SVO)
- Question formation

These are **structural rules**, like:
- Musical notation (notes, rhythm, key)
- Chess rules (how pieces move)
- Haiku structure (5-7-5 syllables)

**Constraints enable creativity within form!**

### Example: Subject-Verb Agreement

```python
# NOT a template, it's a RULE:
if subject == 'I':
    verb = 'am'  # not 'is'
elif subject in ['he', 'she', 'it']:
    verb = 'is'  # not 'are'
elif subject in ['they', 'we', 'you']:
    verb = 'are'  # not 'is'
```

This doesn't dictate WHAT to say, only HOW to say it correctly!
